## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Close the last Tier-1 P1 pull: turn the three placeholder seats (currently tsumogiri/auto-pass) into non-cheating opponents that play a hand start to finish. The play has three load-bearing pieces that belong together as one bigger-picture move — (1) a SeatView projection in core that hard-codes fair-play by construction: exactly what one seat legitimately sees (own hand + drawn tile, all ponds, all exposed melds, dora indicators, wall count, turn/phase) and nothing hidden; (2) a shanten datum in core so 'competent' has a definition — standard form + chiitoitsu + kokushi, minimum of the three — this is the same efficiency module the Tier-2 teaching prompts will read later, and the P5 shanten-correctness crown; and (3) a pure, deterministic bot policy `(SeatView, offered legal actions) → chosen action` wired through the existing drive seam for S/W/N, leaving East human. Why now: every other P1 organ (wall, deal, draw/discard, calls, agari) already exists behind the engine; the table is inert without opponents, and this is the pull that makes a beginner's next hand an actual game rather than a solitaire drill.

## Value to the design

Realizes the 'three competent, non-cheating bots' half of P1 Playability — a legal, finishable hand played against opponents who call and win — while landing the P5 shanten-correctness crown (a property-tested calculator) and preserving the P5 AI-vs-AI determinism invariant (no RNG in the policy, ties broken by a documented rule, so seeded hands replay identically). Fair-play is structural, not policed: a bot literally cannot read hidden state because SeatView never exposes it. Seeds P2 by producing the shanten module the teaching prompts will consume.

## Done looks like

Seed a game and hand three seats to the policy (East human, or all four botted in a test-only harness): those seats play a legal hand to completion on their own — drawing, discarding, taking shanten-cutting yaku-preserving calls, passing claims that would strand a yakuless open hand, and always taking a legal tsumo/ron — and every action they choose is an element of the offered legal set. The same seed replays the same AI-vs-AI hand identically end to end, and a full-botted game provably terminates. The shanten calculator agrees with a brute-force reference across constrained property-test samples and reads 0 exactly at tenpai (agreeing with the enumerated waits). Bots visibly play the three non-East seats autonomously at the existing pace, with no difficulty selector present.
