## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Give the engine the call vocabulary — chi from the left neighbor, pon from any seat, and kan in all three forms (daiminkan, ankan, shouminkan) — as interruption semantics over the existing draw/discard fold. After a discard, eligible seats may claim it; a claim moves the meld to an exposed area, marks the claimed tile in the pond, and jumps the turn to the caller, who then discards. Every kan draws a rinshan replacement from the dead wall, flips a new kan-dora indicator, and shortens the live wall so exhaustive draw still fires at the right moment. Priority is pon/kan over chi when claims collide. The log stays the single authority: it records what happened (never offers), resolution order is deterministic from the record, and illegal claims — wrong tiles, wrong seat, stale discard — throw. legalActions grows to offer claims per eligible seat after a discard plus ankan/shouminkan on the active turn, and the view surfaces call/pass prompts (with chi-variant choice) when the human East may claim. This is the last structural piece of table mechanics before win detection, and it lands now because E-003's tap-to-discard loop just proved the fold-and-offer seam it extends.

## Value to the design

Advances P1 by delivering the 'legal calls (chi/pon/kan)' clause verbatim — the final structural mechanic between the current draw/discard loop and agari. Advances P5 by extending the property-test dynamics to the hardest invariants yet: tile conservation with melds counted (hands + melds + ponds + live + dead == 136), termination with kans consuming the live wall, replay determinism across interruptions, and an illegal-claim mutation matrix — while the agreement suite (every offered action folds; non-offered candidates throw) remains the engine's public contract.

## Done looks like

In a seeded hand, a discard can be legally claimed: the meld appears exposed for the calling seat, the claimed tile is marked in the pond, and play resumes from the caller — with any kan drawing rinshan, flipping a new kan-dora indicator, and hastening ryuukyoku correctly. Replaying the hand's log reproduces the identical table state, illegal claims throw, the extended property suite (conservation-with-melds, termination-with-kans, determinism, mutation matrix) is green, and the human player sees a call/pass prompt — including a chi-variant choice — in the running app.
