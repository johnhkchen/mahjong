## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Two residual noise sources after E-011's legibility work. (1) Mechanical: consecutive
claim windows can mount ~250ms apart, so a tap (or the second half of a phone
double-tap) aimed at window 1 lands on window 2's buttons — the misland behind the
"double action prompts" reports. Freshly-mounted prompts must ignore activations for
their entry beat. (2) Attentional: the player is prompted for EVERY legal call,
including chis/pons the bot policy itself would never take; each one pauses the game.
Default the prompts to plausibly-useful calls (the policy's own shanten-cutting /
yaku-preserving judgment, wins always prompted), with a persisted "prompt every legal
call" toggle for purists — the learner keeps seeing real decisions, not noise.

## Done looks like

Interaction tests prove a tap within the entry beat of a fresh prompt is inert (and
that the beat matches the CSS entry transition, `prefers-reduced-motion` collapsing
both); with default settings a call the policy would decline never prompts (the window
auto-passes through the existing seam) while wins and useful calls always prompt; the
"prompt every legal call" toggle persists via localStorage alongside the terminology
key and restores the old behavior; both terminologies covered; suite green.

## Context & constraints

View/drive-only — `legalActions` still enumerates everything (core untouched); the
filter is a prompt-level presentation choice reusing the exported policy judgment, not
new legality logic. The guard must not delay the win button's availability beyond the
beat. No new dependencies. Out of scope: changing claim priority, engine gates, sound.
