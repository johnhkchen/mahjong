## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Every other organ of a hand exists — wall, deal, calls, agari, the full standard+yakuman yaku catalog (`src/core/yaku.ts`, `yakuman.ts`) — but a win today just ends the hand with a named yaku list and nothing changes hands. This epic closes that gap on two fronts that belong together: (1) a scorer that takes a win's yaku names + dora/kan-dora + the winning decomposition and produces han, fu (by the standard table: base 20, closed-ron 10, wait/pair/set fu, tsumo 2, pinfu's 20/30 split, chiitoitsu's fixed 25, round-up-to-10), and points through the full dealer/non-dealer × ron/tsumo payment table up through yakuman, plus ryuukyoku's noten-bappu 3000 exchange; and (2) extending the record contract one level up from hand to GAME — starting scores of 25000, an ordered list of hand records, seat scores derived by folding outcomes through the scorer (same fold-only authority the hand record already guarantees) — so 'next hand' deals fresh from a derived seed with scores carried, the dealer repeating on a dealer win (renchan) or rotating otherwise, with seat winds rotating correctly (round wind holds East) and 'new game' resetting to 25000 each. `yaku.ts`'s own header comment names this boundary explicitly: 'han and fu belong to the scoring epic and appear nowhere in this API' — this is that epic.

## Value to the design

Realizes P5's named property-test crown (the han/fu → points grid, point conservation across every settlement, pinfu/chiitoi fu invariants, dora-never-a-yaku, scored full-game fold determinism) for the first time — the axis the charter says a one-shot structurally cannot reach. Realizes P1 by making a hand actually cost and pay, and closes the loop so a hand is no longer a dead end: the game continues. Realizes P2 by making the score breakdown the yaku lesson made concrete — a beginner sees exactly which named yaku earned which han, how dora counted, how fu was itemized, and what that priced out to.

## Done looks like

Playing any hand to a win or to ryuukyoku ends with an on-table score breakdown — each yaku named with its han, dora counted, fu itemized, and the points line (e.g. '30fu 4han 7700' or 'mangan 8000') — and the four seat scores update correctly by the standard payment table (dealer/non-dealer × ron/tsumo, mangan through yakuman limits with standard rounding) or, on a draw, by noten-bappu between tenpai and noten seats. Tapping 'next hand' deals a fresh hand from a derived per-hand seed with scores carried forward, the dealer repeating on a dealer win and rotating otherwise (seat-wind yakuhai following the rotated seats, round wind held at East), and 'new game' resets every seat to 25000. The full han/fu × fu-step property grid holds against the published payment table for both dealer-ness and both win modes including limit rounding, every settlement (wins and noten-bappu) conserves points to zero, and a multi-hand AI-vs-AI game folds deterministically end to end.

## Context & constraints

Grounds directly on the existing engine: `src/core/yaku.ts` (standard yaku name catalog, closed set, header comment explicitly defers han/fu to this epic), `src/core/yakuman.ts` (yakuOf aggregator, the one-yaku win gate), and `src/core/record.ts` (TableState.win / hand-level fold — a hand ends at 'agari' or ryuukyoku today with no concept of a game spanning hands). PREREQUISITE (not hidden scope): this epic consumes the existing yaku detection and win/ryuukyoku fold as-is — it does not redefine yaku logic, only prices what's already detected. View surface to extend: `src/app/Table.svelte` / `src/app/drive.ts`'s existing hand-end presentation grows the score breakdown and next-hand/new-game controls; no new view scaffolding beyond that. Out of scope, per the signal: the riichi mechanic and anything riichi-dependent (stick deposits, ippatsu, uradora), honba counters, full hanchan/tonpuusen wind progression and game-end/placement/uma, busting rules, persistence, attract mode, difficulty. Keep set-count/hand-size as named constants (Taiwan 16-tile guardrail, owner 2026-07-02).
