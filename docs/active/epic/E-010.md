## Intent — the bigger-picture play

The game's move and term labels are romanized Japanese (CHI, PON, KAN, RON, TSUMO, RIICHI,
RYUUKYOKU). Offer a Traditional Chinese characters option — 吃 碰 槓 胡 自摸 立直 流局 聽牌
振聽, seat labels 東/南/西/北, and the score-screen terms — as a view-only label dictionary
in `src/app/`. A small header toggle switches terminology; the choice persists as one
`localStorage` key; the default stays the current romanized labels.

## Done looks like

Tapping the terminology toggle relabels every game term (call/win prompts, seat names,
hand-end and score-screen terms) to Traditional Chinese and back; the choice survives a
reload via one `localStorage` key; `src/core/` is byte-for-byte untouched; all SSR/drive
tests are green in both terminologies (structure/token matching, or pinning the default).

## Context & constraints

View-only: core speaks typed action/state names; the view maps them to labels through one
dictionary module. No i18n framework, no translation of teaching prose beyond game terms,
no yaku-name translations beyond the score screen's term labels, no core changes. First
`localStorage` use in the app — keep it to the single key (broader persistence is its own
Tier-3 signal).
