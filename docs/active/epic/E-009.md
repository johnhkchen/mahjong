## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Riichi is the one mechanic the definition-of-done names by name, and right now it doesn't exist: yaku.ts's own comments say the riichi family (riichi, double riichi, ippatsu) is 'deliberately absent: no riichi declaration exists in the action vocabulary,' han.ts says 'No ura-dora: riichi is not yet in the action [log],' settlement.ts says 'No honba, no riichi sticks: neither exists in TableState yet,' and legal.ts already carves out the seam ('Temporary and riichi furiten are the riichi ticket's extensions, not this rule'). This epic closes that seam end to end: give closed-tenpai seats a real riichi HandAction that locks the hand and stakes a stick, price the riichi yaku family and uradora through the existing E-008 settlement pipeline with the stick pot zero-sum across ryuukyoku, complete furiten with its riichi and temporary variants (both derived from the log, not stored), teach a simple bot policy to declare when legal, and surface the whole thing legibly — tenpai/riichi prompt, furiten badge, yakuless-completion notice, shanten hint — so a beginner sees the stakes and calls riichi on purpose instead of by accident.

## Value to the design

Realizes P2 directly (the crown invariant is literally 'you're tenpai — declare riichi?' plus legible yaku/furiten), P1 by completing the legal-action surface for a real Riichi hand (declaration is core to the ruleset the game claims to play), and P5 by extending the property-tested scoring crown (han/fu → points) to riichi/ippatsu/uradora and by keeping AI-vs-AI seeded simulation deterministic once riichi enters bot play.

## Done looks like

A closed-tenpai seat (player or bot) can declare riichi on a legal discard; the hand locks to tsumogiri-only and kan-after-riichi is disallowed; a 1000-point stick moves from the seat's score into a pot that a later winner takes and that a ryuukyoku correctly carries forward via GameRecord; a riichi win prices riichi/double-riichi/ippatsu/uradora through settlement.ts with every settlement zero-sum; a passed ron while in riichi seals ron permanently and a passed ron outside riichi seals it until the seat's next draw, both derived from the action log with no new stored flags; legalActions offers riichi exactly when legal and the agreement/property suite covers it; bots declare riichi under a simple documented rule without breaking seeded AI-vs-AI determinism; and the teaching view shows a tenpai/riichi prompt, a furiten badge naming the sealing discard, a yakuless-completion notice, and a subtle pre-tenpai shanten hint.

## Context & constraints

Grounded directly in the current src/core/ tree: yaku.ts, han.ts, settlement.ts, and legal.ts all contain explicit comments marking riichi/ippatsu/uradora/riichi-sticks/riichi-furiten as deferred extension points, and legal.ts already implements basic discard furiten (discardFuriten) that this epic must extend, not replace. Prerequisites this epic builds on (already merged, not in scope to redo): the E-008 GameState/GameRecord fold with dealer rotation and renchan (game.ts), the settlement engine with baseOf/roundUp100/ronDeltas/tsumoDeltas (settlement.ts), and the shanten calculator (shanten.ts) the teaching hint reuses. Out of scope per the demand signal: defense/safe-tile hints, post-hand review, hanchan wind progression, persistence, attract mode, difficulty tuning — none of those are touched here. Decomposition (left to DecomposeEpic, not authored here) is expected to land close to three stories / ~8 tickets — riichi-engine (declaration+lock+stick fold, riichi yaku family + uradora pricing, furiten completion + property suite), bot-and-dynamics (policy rule + riichi'd AI-vs-AI determinism), teaching-view (tenpai/riichi prompt, furiten badge, yakuless notice, shanten hint) — which matches E-008's own scale (3 stories, 8 tickets), so this is right-sized against project precedent rather than inflated.
