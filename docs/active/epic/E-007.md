_Proposed by Vend's `propose-epic` play._

## Intent — the bigger-picture play

The owner playtested and asked for feel: the table works but doesn't look or move like a real parlor. Give the existing view an original drawn-from-scratch inline-SVG tile set (ivory face, beveled body, engraved glyphs; kanji man numerals, coin pin, bamboo sou, wind/dragon honors, plus the eight flowers as decorative-only assets pre-paying the committed Taiwan 16-tile variant), a portrait mobile-first layout with the hand in the thumb zone and all four ponds/melds/dora/prompts reachable, and cheap CSS-only motion for draw, discard-to-pond, meld exposure, and hand-end reveal — plus display-only hand sorting that still maps taps back to the right tile id. This is where the no-commercial-tileset rule (P4) is asserted and made visible, and it's the owner's direct, current ask.

## Value to the design

Realizes P4 (feel and original art) end-to-end for the first time — the mobile table, tile sorting, and call/reveal motion the charter names, on a fully original asset set — while actively protecting P3 by holding the whole art pack inside the single-file build under the size gate. All of it is a caller of the existing fold: no new authoritative state, view-only.

## Done looks like

On a phone in portrait, a beginner sees an original Taiwan-style tile set (no scanned/cloned commercial art), plays a whole hand one-thumbed with no horizontal scroll and ~44px touch targets, reads all four ponds/melds/dora at a glance, sees draws/discards/claims/hand-end animate smoothly (~150–250ms, no jank), and their concealed hand shows sorted while taps still discard the intended tile — with `dist/index.html` still well under the ~300KB single-file gate, and svelte-check + all existing SSR/drive tests green. src/core/ is byte-for-byte untouched and no runtime dependency was added.

## Context & constraints

View-only epic: src/core/ must stay untouched (the app keeps deriving table state from the pure fold; sorting and animation are presentation over the action-log-derived state, and the tap→tile-id mapping is the one correctness risk to guard). No new runtime dependencies; CSS transitions only, no animation library. Respects non-goals N1–N4 and charter rejects — no sound, haptics, dark mode, settings/menu beyond what exists, licensing-text UI, or attract-mode work, and explicitly no riichi-stick animation (no riichi mechanic is dealt yet). Flowers (梅蘭菊竹 + 春夏秋冬) are drawn as DECORATIVE assets only and are NOT dealt while the ruleset is Riichi — they prepay the committed Taiwan 16-tile post-DoD variant (owner, 2026-07-02); do not wire them into deal/draw. PREREQUISITE: an existing rendered table view in src/app/ to reskin and re-layout (the drive/SSR tests imply one exists). PE-7 flag: this is on the LARGE side of one epic — the from-scratch tile-art pack (34 Riichi faces + 8 flowers + tile backs) is nearly epic-sized on its own, and layout and motion are each substantial. DecomposeEpic should expect roughly three stories (art pack / mobile layout / sort+motion) and may reasonably split the art pack into its own epic if a single ~2-hour block can't hold it; the size-gate check belongs in whichever story lands the SVG pack.
