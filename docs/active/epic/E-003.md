## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Give the engine its beating heart: a real turn cycle over the action-log contract. Widen the empty HandAction vocabulary in src/core/record.ts with the first live actions — draw and discard — and replace foldRecord's guard with a per-action step function, so East→South→West→North turns fold deterministically from the record: the active seat draws from the live wall, discards (from hand or tsumogiri) into their own ordered pond, and the turn advances until wall exhaustion ends the hand in exhaustive draw. Expose the other half of the engine contract — legal actions for the current state — so the app and future bots consume legality from the engine instead of inventing it. On the table, the human plays East by tapping a tile to discard; the other three seats tsumogiri as a stopgap, and all four ponds plus the wall counter update purely via the fold.

## Value to the design

P1: this is the step from 'a dealt hand rendered' to 'a finishable hand played' — draw, discard, turn order, and a guaranteed ending are the minimum loop everything else (calls, riichi, agari, bots) attaches to. P5: it converts property rigor from static wall facts to dynamics over a real action vocabulary — tile conservation across arbitrary legal sequences, fold determinism, and illegal-action rejection become the exhibit. Exposing legal-actions completes the engine's public contract (log in → legal actions / next state out) that architecture.md declares load-bearing.

## Done looks like

A seeded hand plays from deal to exhaustive draw entirely as a fold over its record: the human (East) taps tiles to discard, the other three seats tsumogiri automatically, the four ordered ponds and wall counter update on screen, and ryuukyoku fires exactly when the live wall empties. `just test` is green with property tests asserting conservation (hands + ponds + live + dead == 136 distinct tile ids) across random legal action sequences, deterministic re-folds, and throws on wrong-seat / tile-not-in-hand / illegal-draw actions.

## Context & constraints

Grounds on what E-002 left on main: foldRecord as the core contract entrypoint with an empty HandAction vocabulary (T-002-01-04) and the dealt hand + dora indicator rendered via the fold (T-002-02-01) — both are prerequisites and are already merged. TableState grows per-seat ponds, a turn pointer, the drawn tile, and a phase/ended marker; nothing but the fold is authoritative (architecture invariant). The three non-East seats tsumogiri via a deliberate placeholder — competent bots are a separate future epic, and the legal-actions surface built here is exactly what they will consume. Out of scope, per the signal: calls (chi/pon/kan), riichi, agari/win detection, shanten, yaku, scoring, AI bots, text serialization of the log, and persistence. No non-goals touched (no server, no accounts, no multiplayer). Sizing: dense but coherent — one engine step function + legality surface + its property suite + a thin tap-to-discard view is at the upper edge of a single ~2-hour block; if it strains in decomposition, the view wiring is the natural seam to split at, not the engine.
